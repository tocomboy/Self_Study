History of Gyeongsan

1. Center of Buddhism during the Three Kingdoms Period

Gyeongsan was an important center of Buddhism during the Silla era. It is the hometown of the Three Saints (Samseonghyeon): Wonhyo, Seol Chong, and Iryeon.
Wonhyo was a Buddhist monk who greatly contributed to the development of Korean Buddhism, making Buddhist teachings more accessible to the general public.
His son, Seol Chong, was a scholar who advanced Confucian education and philosophy in Silla. Iryeon, a monk from the Goryeo period, wrote the Samguk Yusa, a collection of historical tales, which preserved Korea's early history and Buddhism. Gyeongsan’s deep connection to Buddhism continues to be a defining feature of its historical heritage.


More information of Three saints

2. Educational and Scholarly Hub during the Joseon Dynasty

During the Joseon Dynasty, Gyeongsan further developed as a center of education and scholarship.
Confucianism flourished in this region, and many Confucian academies and schools were established, producing notable scholars.
The Gyeongsan Hyanggyo (Confucian school) was one of the key institutions that promoted Confucian education.
Throughout this period, Gyeongsan became known for its commitment to Confucian values and intellectual growth.


More information of Gyeongsan Hyanggyo

3. Modern Center of Education and Industry

In modern times, Gyeongsan has evolved into a city that blends education and industry.
With prominent universities such as Yeungnam University and Daegu University located here, Gyeongsan has become a thriving educational hub.
Additionally, its proximity to Daegu has made Gyeongsan a vital part of the regional economy, with industries like automotive and electronic parts manufacturing flourishing.
This combination of education and industry has attracted a young population and fostered a vibrant economy, making Gyeongsan an emerging city of growth and opportunity.
